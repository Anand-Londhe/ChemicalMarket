Petcoke gasification is a process that converts petroleum coke (petcoke), a byproduct of oil refining, into syngas (a mixture of carbon monoxide and hydrogen). This syngas can be used to generate electricity, produce hydrogen, or serve as feedstock for chemicals and fuels. The process involves reacting petcoke with oxygen and steam under high temperatures, breaking down the carbon structure into useful gaseous components. It is a viable solution for utilizing heavy residues from refineries, reducing waste, and promoting energy efficiency.
